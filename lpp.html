What is an LPP?

A licensed legal provider for limited Utah matters.

A Licensed Paralegal Practitioner is not a lawyer, but is licensed in Utah to provide limited legal services in approved practice areas.

Where an LPP fits

An LPP is a mid-level legal provider — more than a paralegal, but not a fully practicing attorney. The purpose of the program is to improve access to justice for people who may not need, or cannot afford, full attorney representation.

Current practice areas

Utah LPPs may be licensed in family law, debt collection, and landlord-tenant matters. Amanda’s site focuses on the areas she currently serves.

How an LPP can help

An LPP can interview clients, review and explain documents, help prepare court-approved forms, gather information, file forms for clients, communicate with opposing counsel, and assist with settlement documents within the authorized scope.

Limits matter

Some cases require an attorney. This site should help potential clients understand the limits of LPP services and ask the right questions during a consultation.

Required clarity: A Licensed Paralegal Practitioner is not a lawyer and is not licensed to provide legal services without limitation.
