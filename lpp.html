Understanding LPP Services in Utah

Your Utah Licensed Paralegal Practitioner: Focused Legal Support.

A Licensed Paralegal Practitioner is not a lawyer, but is licensed in Utah to provide limited legal services in approved practice areas.

Where an LPP fits

An LPP bridges the gap between paralegals and attorneys, offering licensed, limited legal services. This program is designed to make justice more accessible and affordable for Utahns facing specific legal challenges.

Current practice areas

LPPs in Utah are authorized to practice in areas like family law, debt collection, and landlord-tenant matters. Amanda focuses on family law, debt collection, and mediation.

How an LPP can help

LPPs can assist with client interviews, document review and preparation, form filing, information gathering, and settlement negotiation, all within their authorized practice scope.

Limits matter

It's crucial to understand the boundaries of LPP services. This page, and your consultation, will help you determine if LPP assistance is the right fit for your case.

Required clarity: A Licensed Paralegal Practitioner is not a lawyer and is not licensed to provide legal services without limitation.
